Immigration Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a visa?

A visa is an official document that lets you enter a country you're not a citizen of. It’s usually a stamp or sticker in your passport. The visa gives you permission to visit a country for a specific reason, like tourism, work, or study, and for a set amount of time.

What types of visas are there?

There are two types of American visas: immigrant visas for permanent residency and nonimmigrant visas for temporary visits.

What are the immigrant visa types?

  • IR (Immediate Relative) Visa: For close family members of U.S. citizens (e.g., spouse, fiancé).
  • EB (Employment-Based) Visas: For workers with special skills or investors:
    • EB-1: Priority workers
    • EB-2: Professionals with advanced degrees
    • EB-5: Investors
  • DV (Diversity Visa): A lottery for individuals from countries with few immigrants to the U.S.
  • SIV (Special Immigrant Visa): For religious workers or individuals who worked for the U.S. government in Iraq/Afghanistan.

What are the nonimmigrant visa types?

  • B: For business (B-1), tourism (B-2), or both.
  • F: For students studying at a school
  • M: For students at a trade or vocational school
  • J: For people in exchange programs (like cultural or educational exchanges)
  • H: For temporary workers:
    • H-1B: For jobs that need special skills
    • H-2A/H-2B: For farm workers and other temporary workers
  • L: For workers moving to the U.S. within the same company.
  • O: For people with extraordinary skills or talents
  • P: For athletes, artists, and entertainers
  • R: For religious workers
  • I: For foreign media representatives.
  • K: For fiancé(e)s of U.S. citizens to come to get married in the U.S.

What is an Asylum Seeker or Asylee?

An Asylum Seeker is someone who has fled their home country due to fear of persecution based on race, religion, nationality, or political beliefs. If granted asylum in the U.S., they can live, work, and eventually apply for permanent residency.

What is a Conditional Permanent Resident?

A Conditional Permanent Resident is someone who obtained permanent residency through marriage to a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident. This status is valid for two years and can be extended if a joint petition is filed. It may be revoked if the petition is not filed or if the marriage is considered fraudulent.

What is DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals)?

DACA protects certain undocumented individuals who were brought to the U.S. as children from deportation. DACA recipients can work legally in the U.S., but they do not have a direct path to permanent residency.

What is a Family-Based Visa?

A Family-Based Visa allows foreign nationals to immigrate to the U.S. based on their relationship with a U.S. citizen or permanent resident. There are two types:

  • Immediate Relatives (spouses, unmarried children under 21, parents of U.S. citizens).
  • Family Preference Categories (unmarried adult children, spouses and children of permanent residents, etc.).

Who are Green Card Holders (Lawful Permanent Residents)?

Green Card Holders are non-citizens who are authorized to live and work permanently in the U.S. They can apply for U.S. citizenship after meeting residency and other requirements.

What is a Naturalized Citizen?

A Naturalized Citizen is someone who was born outside the U.S. but has legally become a U.S. citizen through the naturalization process, which includes meeting residency requirements, passing a test, and taking an oath of allegiance.

What is Refugee Status?

Refugee Status is granted to individuals who apply for asylum from outside the U.S., often through a refugee camp. The process involves organizations like the UNHCR and national refugee programs.

What is a Student Visa (F-1/M-1)?

A Student Visa allows foreign nationals to study in the U.S. F-1 visas are for academic studies, and M-1 visas are for vocational programs. Both have restrictions on employment.

What is a T Visa (T Non-immigrant Status)?

A T Visa helps protect victims of severe human trafficking (sex or labor trafficking). It offers a path to permanent residency after three years, provided the individual cooperates with law enforcement or is under 18.

What is a Tourist Visa (B-2)?

A Tourist Visa allows people to visit the U.S. temporarily for tourism, family visits, or medical treatment. Employment is not permitted under this visa.

What is a U Visa?

A U Visa is for victims of certain crimes (such as abuse) who assist law enforcement in investigations. It allows the victim and their immediate family to live and work in the U.S. for up to four years, with a path to permanent residency.

What is a Worker Visa?

A Worker Visa (such as H-1B, L-1, O-1) lets foreign nationals work in the U.S. temporarily in specific job categories. These visas require employer sponsorship and are typically limited in duration.
